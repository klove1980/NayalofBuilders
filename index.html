Why ADUs? The Math Makes Sense

Accessory Dwelling Units aren't just a trend—they're a smart financial decision for Maryland homeowners. Here's the clear math behind why ADUs work.

💰

Extra Rental Income

Average 2BR/1BA (non-apartment) rent in Southern Maryland: $1,000–$1,600/mo

At $1,400/mo, that's $16,800 per year in passive income.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Multigenerational Privacy

Perfect for parents in their golden years or adult children needing a head start—everyone gets their own space while staying close.

📈

Boost Property Value

ADUs significantly increase your home's resale value. You're not just adding square footage—you're adding income potential that buyers recognize.

🏡

Most Affordable Path to Rental Real Estate

No land purchase required. Often no land clearing needed. Build on property you already own and start generating income faster.

💳

Financing Options

We work with trusted financing partners to make ADU construction accessible. Explore your financing options or learn more about RenoFi contractor financing.

🏖️

Vacation Rental Income

Rent your ADU on platforms like Airbnb or VRBO. Maryland's location near D.C., Baltimore, and the Chesapeake Bay makes it ideal for short-term stays, often earning more per night than long-term rentals.

Maryland ADU Basics: What You Need to Know

Zoning Basics

Maryland's ADU regulations vary by county. In Southern Maryland, many counties allow ADUs on single-family lots, but requirements differ. We'll help you navigate your specific county's rules.

County-by-County ADU Rules

Each Maryland county has its own ADU regulations. St. Mary's, Calvert, and Charles counties each have different size limits, setback requirements, and permit processes. We know the local rules.

Permit Timelines

Typical ADU permits in Maryland take 4-8 weeks, depending on the county and project complexity. We handle all permit applications and keep you updated throughout the process.

Typical ADU Sizes

Most Maryland ADUs range from 400–1,000 sq ft. This size range maximizes rental income potential while staying within zoning requirements and budget constraints.
